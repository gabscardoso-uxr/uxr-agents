Exec Summary

Strategic Summary

The week-one adoption signal is likely curiosity, not value — the real question is not which of the four hypotheses is correct, but whether the assistant is solving a problem users return to, and if so, for whom.

For the Designer

Where assumptions and reality diverge, hypotheses to design against, segments, workarounds.

Where Assumptions and Reality Diverge

What the team or industry assumesWhat may actually be true
High week-1 adoption = product-market fitWeek-1 usage reflects curiosity and in-app promotion, not durable utility (hypothesis)
Drop-off indicates a failure (onboarding, trust, quality)Drop-off may be rational — users tried it, got the answer, and don't need it weekly (hypothesis)
Users trust the assistant because they trust the appTrust in the app brand does not transfer to AI outputs about their money (hypothesis)
All four candidate causes are equally likelyOne or two will dominate per segment; they are not equally weighted (hypothesis)
Action Required → Designer should hold concept work until the diagnostic is complete. Early concepting against the wrong hypothesis creates sunk-cost bias in the Week 3 decision.

Hypotheses to Design Against

  1. Lapsed users tried the assistant for a high-stakes money question, received an output they could not verify, and disengaged — which means the design must make source, confidence, and verification visible before users act.
  2. Lapsed users completed a one-off task and had no recurring reason to return — which means the design must either create recurring triggers tied to user events or accept an occasional-use pattern and stop optimizing for retention.
  3. Returning users are a narrow behavioral segment whose needs differ from mass-adopters — which means the design should specialize for that segment rather than generalize for the week-1 crowd.

Flag ⚠ Workarounds are unknown and must be surfaced in fieldwork. Candidates to probe: in-app search, support chat, external search engines, financial advisors, spreadsheets, other fintech apps. Designing without knowing what the assistant competes with risks building against a weaker substitute than the one users actually use.

What Is Irreversible

  • Data schema for conversation logs and user feedback signals — changing this later breaks longitudinal measurement
  • Compliance posture on what the assistant asserts about user accounts vs. general information — rolling back is costly
  • Integration surface (which in-app contexts invoke the assistant) — widely deployed entry points are hard to retract

What Research Cannot Answer

Context Research can identify which of the four hypotheses is driving drop-off and for whom. It cannot determine: whether a given output was factually correct (requires eval infrastructure); whether model quality can be improved to the level users need; compliance and regulatory boundaries on output scope; cost-per-session economics of proposed improvements.

Research Plan

For whoever is running the study. Full detail.

Existing Signals to Review First

  1. Session logs segmented by week-1 returners vs. lapsed users — task type, session length, completion, feedback signals
  2. Funnel from assistant entry point → first question → outcome → re-entry
  3. Existing feedback mechanisms: thumbs ratings, in-app NPS, support tickets mentioning the assistant
  4. App store reviews and social mentions from the launch window
  5. Any prior qualitative research on the parent app's trust, onboarding, or AI feature expectations
Flag ⚠ If session logs do not distinguish task type or outcome, the cohort analysis cannot narrow the four hypotheses. Name the gap before Week 1 and instrument if possible.

Research Questions

Primary question: What is driving the drop-off in AI assistant usage after week one, and for which users?

  1. What job did lapsed users come to the assistant to do, and what happened in that session?
  2. What distinguishes users who return from those who don't — task type, outcome, context, or prior trust in AI?
  3. Where, specifically, does trust break down (before asking, during output, after acting)?
  4. What do lapsed users use instead today, and how does the assistant compare?
